The Servo By the Sea Written by the Goblin Gophers Chapters Chapter 1 - Ethan and his Missing Bird Chapter 2 - The Hut and the Dinghy Chapter 3 - Danger on the Ship Chapter 4 - Salazar Returns Chapter 5 - ILL-Eagle Confession Chapter 6 - Ethan and Cooper Chapter 7 - Aftermath Chapter 1 - Ethan and his Missing Bird “Salazar. SALAZAR! SALAZAR!” All of Kingscliff could hear Ethan’s voice as he rode his pizza delivery bike down the Eastern Australian coast, and yelled in search of his pet bird. Ethan’s ruby-coloured bike shook under the weight of each of his frantic movements, desperate and he trembled at the thought of losing Salazar, the eagle he had owned for almost a decade. His search had been going for several hours, ever since Ethan had awoken that morning to see Salazar’s chain snapped and the eagle missing from his cage. Despite his worries, Ethan was hopeful that someone would recover his eagle, or that he’d be able to follow in the direction and eventually find his bird. While riding down the rocky, gravel street after a second hour of searching, Ethan passed by a tall, bulky man, who wore a surfing wetsuit. The man had a lengthy blond mullet, and his eyes were covered with black sunglasses. He was midway through waxing a green and gold longboard, positioned just outside of a service station. The service station was small, which fitted for a coastal Australian town. It was made half of grey concrete and half of big open windows, with about 6 petrol pumps lining the side. The exposed windows glistened directly into Ethan’s eyes. Next to the Service station, there was a small car repair station, its door extended upwards, which revealed a suspended car in the middle of a repair. Against the brick wall connecting the service and car repair stations, the man had been waxing his board. “Hey mate, do you need any help?” The surfer called out across the road, as he was concerned at Ethan’s desperate cries. “MY BIRD. MY BIRD’S GONE MISSING!” Ethan halted his bike in the middle of the road, alerted by the stranger calling at him from the service station. He set his bike down outside of the station and then approached the surfer quickly, panting and sweating to catch his breath. “MY - MY - MY - MY BIRD. SALAZAR! HIS CHAIN BROKE AND HE ESCAPED! I'VE SPENT AGES LOOKING FOR HIM. PLEASE HELP ME. I WANT MY BIRD BACK!” “Hey mate, slow down a bit. Let's go inside so you can catch your breath and calm down.” Cooper left his board against the wall, and put down his wax comb in a bucket on the floor, and then proceeded to enter the building. Ethan hesitantly followed the surfer, who introduced himself as Cooper, who had been living in Kingscliff for the past 22 years. Once they were inside the station after introductions, Ethan briefly went over and purchased a cold bottle of Bunderberg Ginger Beer, which he rested on his forehead to cool down. Cooper used the opportunity to go over to the cleaning section, and purchased a sponge, wax, and an extra wax comb. As the two left the building together, Cooper sat down next to Ethan on the sidewalk. “Tell me what happened to your bird.” “Well, Salazar stays in his cage on my balcony during the night. There's never been an issue at all with disappearances in the past 10 years. Any time he disappears, he usually returns within a couple minutes. That's how he’s been trained since pretty much his birth.” “Do you have any idea what might have happened then?” “Well, I’m not too sure. His chain probably just snapped, and he got startled and has been flying east ever since. At least, that's what I heard from some people who spotted him. “ “Well, we're about as far Eastwards as we can get. Anything beyond this is just ocean all the way up to freakin’ New Zealand.” Ethan’s eyes sank immediately. Slowly, all his hope was fading as he realised it would probably be impossible to find his childhood pet. At that moment, Cooper became invested in helping the distressed boy. “Do you really want to find this eagle, Ethan?” Cooper asked. “Yeah I do, please, can you help me?” “Ok then, follow me. I’ve got a dinghy down by the bay that we can go out on, try and catch your bird before he gets too far out. Maybe he’s just catching a few fish to eat.” Chapter 2 - The Hut and the Dinghy Ethan trailed after Cooper down the scorching, grey footpath, the plastic of their thongs slapping into their feet. They sprinted through the burning hot sand that scraped at their feet, and that got in their thongs. They took slight stops on the way to shake the sand out of their thongs. They both panted, sweat soaking through their shirts and making them see-through, until they reached an old, dingy hut with a weathered, rotting wooden door. The shack was made of rusty corrugated iron and smelled of saltwater but as Cooper reached for the corroded handle and pushed it down he found that it did not budge. He reached into his pocket and felt around for his keychain, quickly locating the loop of keys and pulling them out. He tried each of the keys on the keychain in the rusted old lock on the door, but to no avail, the ancient seal would not budge. “Sorry mate, it's locked.” explained Cooper. “I can try to force it open.” claimed Ethan, hanging onto a glimpse of hope. “Mate, be careful that door is falling apart, it’s on its last hinges. You might get hurt” cautioned Cooper, beginning to worry. “I’ll pay you back for the door later!” Ethan exclaimed as he brutally slammed his shoulder into the rotten planks of the door and kicked at it until it was a pile of splinters scattered helplessly on the sand. “You careless drongo, that's my nan’s shack, you broke it!” “I told you I’d pay you back, I’ll help you rebuild the door, don’t stress man.” The hut was cool as they paced around the damp wooden floor looking for the dinghy. The ocean breeze fluttered in through the opening where the door stood just seconds ago. The overwhelming scent of salt infiltrated their nostrils and made their eyes water as they searched for the inflatable dinghy that would get them out onto the bay to search for Salazar. Ethan didn’t care though, running to a crumpled mess of hot, grimy plastic and starting to search for the blowing hole. ”Quick, help me out here Cooper, Salazar is getting further by the second!” The hot, sticky plastic of the dinghy was stiff in their fingers as Ethan blew up the dinghy and Cooper looked for a pump. Slowly the adrenaline wore off as the minutes passed and Cooper's golden hair stuck to the nape of his neck and sweat coated his body. The sky started to darken as thunderous clouds rolled over the horizon. “We should hurry, it's getting stormy out.” Cooper urged, still annoyed because of the door. The air was hot and sticky and the sky outside was a dark and deep grey. Meanwhile Ethan had managed to find the pump and fill the inflatable dinghy, pulling the plastic taut and firm. Shortly afterwards they lugged the boat back out of the door, the sand still scorched their feet. It had started to cool down and the sun had disappeared completely behind the clouds. Small droplets of rain and sea spray sprinkled their faces as they slowly walked along the beach. Their bodies were heavy with fatigue and the sand slowed them down as they repeatedly tried to shake it out of their thongs. They trudged through the sandy dunes and slowly came to a halt by the water. “Let's set it here, the water seems calm.” Cooper commented contently after looking around. “Alright let's do it.” They gently set the dinghy down onto the cool, glistening water and Cooper held it down while Ethan, uncertain, stepped onto it and sat down. It bobbed back and forth for a few moments and then came to a stop. Cooper stepped in after him and they paused for a second. “We didn't bring any food or water.” Cooper sighed, seemingly annoyed, he flopped down onto the boat and shook his head in disappointment. Ethan pulled out two slightly mushed muesli bars from his back pocket, and tossed one to Cooper. “Is this alright?” “I guess, better than nothing” Cooper mumbled begrudgingly. They both laughed as they set off in the dinghy in search of Salazar, hoping to find him before it got too dark and the tide began to come in. Chapter 3 - Danger on Board “Are we there yet?” Ethan grumbled nauseously once again as the boat pushed and sailed through the rough seas. As the water got rougher and rougher, Ethan complained more and more. The waves battered against the soft hull of the boat, which silenced Ethan’s annoying voice. “Are we there yet?” Ethan asked again, though he knew that the answer would always be no. Ethan dragged his lifeless body and slowly made his way to the edge of the boat. “You seasick mate?” Cooper questioned, his head high as the wind violently blew through his gold and silky mullet. “No…” Ethan reassured him as he swallowed down his vomit in pain. A few hours passed by and the dark sky darkened as the day turned to night. As the time passed by, the hope of finding Salazar slowly diminished. The seas got rougher, the wind more fierce, Ethan more annoying, everything just seemed like it was destined to fail. The waves roared and the rain poured down on the two. The ocean churned violently, threatening to throw Cooper and Ethan out into the deep abyssal ocean below them. Thunder rumbled and lightning crackled through the stormy sky. The boat's tiny outboard rumbled its consistent, mechanical whir and propelled them through the choppy waters. “Look! Ahead! I think there might be something ahead!” Yelled Cooper in desperation. “Yeah! I think you’re right! It’s an island I think! Turn the boat over that way.” Screamed Ethan back over the thunderous crash of the colossal waves. As Cooper reached back to steer the outboard to the side, the engine began to splutter, coughing and then dead. And just like that the pair were left to themselves, dead silent in the roaring of the waves. “What the hell are you doing back there?! Turn the damn engine back on” spat Ethan to Cooper, after he noticed the sudden silence from the back of the boat. “It wasn’t me you idiot, the engine’s dead,” Cooper spurted right back at him, “We’re gonna have to paddle” Ethan muttered a curse under his breath before he leaned over the edge of the boat and paddled with all of his might. Cooper followed his lead and soon they were shouting commands between them in a desperate attempt to steer the tiny craft towards the island in the distance. Ethan’s entire body screamed in pain as his muscles felt like they were about to tear apart. Just as he was about to give up and submit to the storm he felt a rough scraping on the bottom of the boat. “Land!” Ethan screamed at the top of his lungs. “Cooper, land! We made it!” The pair jumped out of the boat and Cooper dragged it ashore. Ethan jumped, celebrated then flopped onto the ground in sheer exhaustion. “C’mon mate, we’re not quite there yet, we still need somewhere to sleep safely, unless you want your mouth to fill with rain while you’re sleeping.” said Cooper, who helped pull him up from the ground then guided him over towards a thicket of palm trees that lined the sandy shore of the island. After they located a few large leaves Cooper laid them down under a large and sheltered tree and then they both slumped to the ground tired, tattered and completely worn out. Sleep came quickly and they were soon both out cold. Chapter 4 - Salazar Returns “What time is it?” Ethan mumbled disorientedly as he rolled around on the sharp spines of the fan palm under him. He rubbed his eyes as he glanced off into the endless, abyssal, black canvas littered with glimmering, bright stars in front of him. While he sank into the moist sand under his toes, he thought about his adventure with Cooper and how they had bonded. How they had become close friends out of such a bizarre scenario. How Cooper had selflessly volunteered himself to help a stranger. He thought back to how they had to break down Cooper’s door to get his boat. And how they pumped up the dinghy together using Cooper’s nan’s ball bump. He thought back to how Cooper comforted him when he got sea sick. He thought about how he had never felt true friendship like this before. Just as he reminisced about the day’s events, a coarse shriek interrupted him. The sound pierced his soul and ignited a fire in his heart. “It could only be one thing. Is it? It must be… Salazar!!!” Ethan excitedly thought. He heard the cry again and knew he had to find him. He jolted and turned to tell Cooper but then froze. As he saw Cooper laying there gracefully, deep in slumber, all of their fond memories came back to him. If Cooper knew that he had actually found Salazar that would mean an end to their story. Ethan could not bear to think of losing him just as their adventure had begun. Thinking impulsively, he decided not to wake Cooper and instead chase after Salazar by himself. After he heard another crow, his eyes honed in on a massive palm tree in the centre of the island. Despite the lashing blows of the wind and the coarse grit of the sand, he hurried over to his beloved pet. The wind howled above him and fruit bats swarmed and squealed as he thundered through the undergrowth. The thorns of the shrubs slashed his legs like razor blades but he did not feel any of it. The only thing on his mind was rescuing Salazar. The frigid oceanic wind blew across his face, the scent of saltwater invading his nose yet he could not stop his crusade of pain. Passion drove him to persist. Ethan had never climbed a tree before this, but he had to. He birred up the tree, and shimmied with an untapped, adrenergic explosion of energy. He grabbed the cold, metallic chain tied around Salazar’s ankle and Ethan gently yanked him down the tree. Salazar tugged back, persistent to fly away but eventually he reached a state of calm. Salazar had recognised and embraced Ethan. A deceitful idea was born in his mind, a way to strengthen his friendship with Cooper. A way to make the adventure just a little bit longer, at the cost of Salazar’s freedom. But only for a few days… a few days… a few days, right? Slowly, Ethan walked towards a small opening in the sole hillock that towered over the island. The ceiling was covered with drooping stalagmites, water dropped at random intervals, which annoyed Ethan. He looked around for a platform to climb up so that he could chain Salazar to the roof. A final pulse of remorse and guilt echoed through Ethan’s subconscious. Was another day, another adventure with Cooper worth it? The guilt dissolved though, and Ethan felt a sense of enlightenment. A sense of friendship. Something he had only ever felt with Salazar, the highfalutin bird of prey that had led him getting bullied and excluded for his entire life. With a smirk he tried to hide, Ethan sprinted back to where they had set up camp to return back to his slumber. He stared off into the endless, abyssal, black canvas littered with glimmering, bright stars, before he slept to the sounds of the tide that crept up the shore. Chapter 5: Ill-eagle Confession As the first rays of dawn streaked across the horizon, Ethan awoke in the cramped confines of their makeshift shelter. He shifted Cooper’s weight off of his body. The storm had shifted direction overnight, and continued past in the way they were headed. Cooper sprung up soon after, determination across his face and an air of unwavering resolve. “Let’s go!” he declared, his voice filled with excitement and vigour. Ethan rubbed his eyes, with the dim morning light shining lightly through. Unenthusiastically, he said, “Maybe Salazar flew back, let’s just go home.” “Nah mate. I can feel it in my nuggets. We’re close. Let’s make a paddle out of these leaves and this wood. We’ll find your bird Ethan.” Ethan reluctantly nodded, keeping his discovery of the bird hidden. Between them, there was this unsaid mutual connection, a connection formed between the past day of events. They were both now over the feeling of unfamiliarity. They swiftly assembled a set of paddles out of leaves, sticks and a bit of vine to tie them together, and pushed the dinghy from the sandy shore, the sea salt misting their faces as they leapt onboard. With each strove of the rough oars, they drifted further away from the island they called home for a night, and into the abyss of the churning water of the storm. Cooper’s arms strained against the oars, his eyes locked onto the horizon. The once calm sea had transformed into a tumultuous expanse of angry waves. Raindrops fell across their skin, like icy spikes that pierced their pores. Ethan’s skin paled as the reality of his situation sank in. “Cooper, let’s go back. It’s too dangerous.” His voice was fearful, but stern. Cooper's gaze remained resolute, as he continued to paddle against the currents that pushed them back. “Seriously. Let’s go back. He’s probably back home. Maybe I missed him somewhere. Let’s go back, we're going to die if we go any further.” “We,” Cooper encouragingly said to Ethan, “are going to find your bird. We’re close, I can feel it..” The storm grew stronger and wrathful. With each passing moment, Ethan’s panic grew. Finally, unable to contain his fear any longer, he erupted and had finally released his dismay. “I FOUND HIM! I found Salazar! I got him, he’s safe… turn back… please. I found him last night and left him in a cave.” Cooper stopped paddling. The thunder, wind, rain, waves, all seemed silent. “Why did you not tell me.?” Ethan started to panic and fear rose within him. “I just wanted to keep going because you were the first friend I’ve had, I just didn’t want to be lonely anymore..” Amidst the raging storm, Ethan sat still on the boat, the rain flowed down his face. Lightning illuminated his lonely figure, a picture of desolation. and revealed his inner turmoil and thunder muffled his sobs of regret, isolation, which dampened his loneliness. With each crashing wave, his tears mingled with the sea, as if nature itself cried with him. The unceasing downpour masked his cries. Shaken by the angry sea, Ethan’s tears became one with the ocean and rain, a poignant symphony of solitude played on the lonely expanse of water. Cooper, with a face mixed with anger and shock, dropped the paddles inside the boat. His anger could be seen on his rough face, his eyes welling with emotion. Without a word, he turned the dinghy around, paddling slowly back to the shore they had left behind. The rain-soaked journey back was filled with a heavy silence, the weight of disappointment and regret hanging between them like a thick fog. The sounds of the thunder, wind, rains, waves all were both silent but exaggerated. When they approached the shore, Cooper wordlessly rowed the dinghy onto the sand line. With a resigned sigh, Cooper told Ethan, “You can stay here. I’m going.” and swiftly paddled off with emotions of anger and disappointment. Ethan’s heart sank as he watched his only friend paddle away. His solitary figure disappeared into the unseeable distance. Again, he walked onto the now familiar island. His eyes stung with regret. Ethan took a moment to reflect on what he had just done. He lost his first and only true friend, all because he wanted to keep him his friend. His shoulders dropped slowly, and the weight of his actions settled on top and mingled with the rain that streamed down his face. In the distance, incandescent lightning illuminated the sky, which casted an eerie glow over the desolated landscape. He watched the clouds above and the waves in front and reminded him of the atrocities he committed to his friend, Cooper. And so, under the watchful gaze of the storm, Ethan began to amble back to the cave, a hollow coastal cavern, cradling echoes of solitude, within it, Ethan’s only remaining friend. Chapter 6: Cooper and Ethan In the meantime, Cooper pondered. He thought of Ethan’s loneliness. What Ethan had done seemed irredeemable. He was a fraud. A liar. A phoney and a cheat. Ethan had put Cooper’s life on the line, for a worthless bird that was already found. A sudden warmth washed over Cooper. A light slowly approached him, a light of sympathy, of friendship, of companionship. Like a beacon that left him enchanted. Without an aim, he stumbled around and looked for Ethan. He saw a vague footstep, an oval in the sand that slowly collapsed inwards. He saw a trail that he followed and hoped to find Ethan. He saw a brightly lit cave, Ethan and Salazar, effulgent as they emerged from the mouth of the cave. At first there was silence, an aura of awkwardness. The cool sand slowly covered their feet as they were still. A transitory state of tranquillity embraced the island. They kept their distance, maybe a metre apart, and walked towards the raft. Frozen, they knew they would have to interact on the raft. Hesitantly they climbed aboard. “You came back for me?” Ethan trembled and tried to muster up the courage to look Cooper in the eye. At first there was no response. The silence was deafening. The timid waves lapped at the sides of the dinghy. Every swipe of the paddle echoed throughout the boat and washed over the duo. Salazar shifted and the rubber squeaked under his weight. Cooper stared into the dusky sky in front of him as he paddled. “‘Cause you’re my mate. And that’s what mates do.” Tears welled up in Ethan’s eyes as he struggled to get any words out. The two sat in silence while they both rowed in increments. The fibres of the leaves which made up the makeshift paddles shredded with every forceful stroke. With every minute that went by the sun seemed to go further down. The sky turned into a vibrant mosaic of oranges, reds and yellows, which turned the horizon into an impressionist landscape. As a ruby hue surrounded them, Ethan smeared his tears across his face and turned to Cooper. “You know, no one’s ever said that to me before. It really means a lot.” “How about when we get back to shore and this whole mess is over we can go out on the beach one day and I’ll teach you how to surf. How does that sound?” “Really? You would do that for me? Aren’t you mad at me because I lied to you and almost got you killed?” “It’s alright, mate. I forgive you. You’re not you when you don’t have your mates.” Once again Ethan began to tear up. As he tried to hold back a waterfall of emotions, Cooper spotted a blip on the horizon. Out of joy and relief he instinctively hugged Ethan. Ethan felt the warmth and comfort of Cooper’s embrace and wanted the moment to last forever. But it was not to be. As soon as his embrace had started, Cooper ripped away his arms and began to row again. The laborious, constant action of rowing had taken a toll on them, but the hope of setting foot back on dry land had reinvigorated them and they began to row with just as much motivation and ambition as they had when they initially set out. “Sploosh. Splosh. Splash.” Stroke after stroke. Push after pull. The last stretch was gruelling on their already drained and tired bodies, but after they mustered all of the energy they could and rowed with all of their strength, they finally made it. As the dinghy brushed up onto the grit of the coast, the duo threw down their palm tree paddles and slumped down onto the beach. Salazar followed closely after and the group drifted to sleep, and they stared up at the clouds that looked as if they were painted into the sky. Chapter 7: Aftermath It has been only 6 months since the two of them became friends, but to them it felt like years. Ethan and Cooper walked their surfboards up the path from the beach, and gulped down a handful of tasty pizza that Ethan made. “The cheese just melts in the mouth.” Cooper had shown Ethan how to surf, and though he wasn’t yet very good, under Cooper’s expert guidance, he had been improving rapidly. Salazar followed them as the pair went to surf again, Cooper yelled constantly at Ethan and told him what to do, and Ethan shouted back and did the complete opposite. “You told me to stand feet in and hands apart when riding the waves!” Ethan exclaimed. “I said hands in and feet apart you dipstick!” Cooper yelled back, furious that Ethan wouldn’t listen. The two of them walked back to the service station to sit down and relax after a long day at the beach. Cooper looked next to the petrol pumps where he saw the grand opening of ‘The Pizza Brothers’. Ethan saw this and comforted him, “Your new shop looks awesome man” he said. Cooper smiled a bit as he laughed in an attempt to hide his smile. Ethan knew he was shy of his big accomplishment, So he didn’t bother him anymore. “You know…” Cooper began to talk after he handed out a piece of paper, “You would make a really good partner for my business.” Ethan skimmed over the paper, it read a contract to be a partner for the business.
